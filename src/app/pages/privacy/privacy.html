Road Travel — Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 18, 2026

Road Travel plans driving routes and shows the weather you'll meet along the way, timed to your estimated arrival at each point. This policy explains what information the app collects, how it is used, who processes it, and the choices you have. We collect only what the app needs to work, we don't show ads or track you across other apps, and we never sell your data.

Summary

  • You can browse and plan with an anonymous session; some features require an account (Apple, Google, or email sign-in), which involves your email address.
  • Your saved trips, stops, and profile (such as vehicle type and preferences) are stored on our backend so they sync across your devices.
  • With your permission, your location is used to plan routes, navigate, and check the weather ahead. It is not sold and we do not keep a location history for advertising.
  • We do not track you across apps or websites, we do not use advertising SDKs, and we do not sell your data.
  • You can export or delete your data at any time (see "Your rights and choices").

Information we collect and use

Account and sign-in

You can use Road Travel with an anonymous session. To save and sync trips, keep Pro across devices, or use account features, you create an account by signing in with Sign in with Apple, Google, or an email magic link. This involves your email address and an account identifier. Authentication is handled by our provider Supabase. We use this to operate your account and secure your data — not for advertising.

Location

With your permission, Road Travel uses your device location to set your starting point, provide turn-by-turn navigation, and check the weather ahead along your route (including while the screen is off during active navigation). To plan a route and match forecasts, the relevant coordinates are sent to our backend and to our mapping, routing, and weather providers (below). We do not keep a location history for advertising or sell your location. You can revoke location access at any time in Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services.

Trips, stops, and profile

When you save a trip, add stops, or set preferences, we store that content — origins, destinations, waypoints, saved trips, vehicle type, onboarding answers, and settings — on our backend, linked to your account, so it syncs across your devices. Address autocomplete is provided through Apple MapKit (on iPhone) or an open geocoding service (on the web) to turn what you type into places.

Weather

Weather forecasts are provided by Apple Weather (WeatherKit). When the app requests a forecast for points along your route, the relevant coordinates are sent to Apple to return the forecast, governed by Apple's privacy policy and the Apple Weather / WeatherKit terms. See Apple's attribution at weatherkit.apple.com/legal-attribution.html.

Maps, search, and routing

Maps, place search, and routing are provided by Apple MapKit (on iPhone) and by Mapbox and open map data (on the web and for multi-stop and point-of-interest routing). The relevant coordinates and search terms are sent to these providers to draw the map, find places, and compute routes.

AI trip briefings

Optional plain-language trip briefings are generated by Anthropic (Claude). The briefing is written from the weather facts already computed for your route (conditions, timing, severity) — not from your identity. We operate this under a zero-retention arrangement: your inputs are not used to train the model and are not retained beyond generating the response.

Subscriptions (Road Travel Pro)

Optional subscriptions are sold through Apple's App Store (StoreKit) and managed with RevenueCat, a subscription-infrastructure provider. To validate purchases and keep your Pro status in sync, your account identifier and purchase/receipt information are processed by Apple and RevenueCat. Apple handles all payment information; Road Travel never sees or stores your payment details.

  • RevenueCat privacy policy: revenuecat.com/privacy
  • Apple's subscription and payment handling is governed by Apple's privacy policy.

Diagnostics

In-app event logging is used only to help the app function. It stays on your device and is not used to build an advertising profile or to track you across other apps or websites.

Service providers (subprocessors)

We share the minimum data needed with providers who process it on our behalf, under contract, to run the app:

  • Supabase — authentication and database for your account and trips (supabase.com/privacy).
  • Google Cloud — hosting for our backend.
  • Apple — weather, maps/search, and App Store subscriptions.
  • Mapbox — web maps, routing, and points of interest (mapbox.com/legal/privacy).
  • RevenueCat — subscription management.
  • Anthropic — AI trip briefings, under a no-training / zero-retention arrangement.

What we do NOT do

  • We do not use third-party advertising or ad tracking.
  • We do not sell or rent your personal information.
  • We do not track you across other apps or websites.
  • We do not use your trips or content to train AI models.

Your rights and choices

You control your data:

  • Export a copy of your data from the app (Settings).
  • Delete your account and its data from the app (Settings); this removes your trips, profile, and account from our backend. Subscription records held by Apple and RevenueCat are retained per their own policies.
  • Revoke location access at any time in your device Settings.
  • Depending on where you live, you may have additional rights (access, correction, deletion, portability). Contact us to exercise them.

Data retention and security

We keep account and trip data while your account is active, and delete it when you delete your account (backups age out on a rolling basis). Data is encrypted in transit (HTTPS/TLS) and at rest by our hosting and database providers.

Children

Road Travel is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect personal information from them.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. Material changes will be reflected here with a new effective date.
